Hospitals

Since the p-value is less than alpha=0.05, we reject the null hypothesis that life expectancy does not increase for people living in zip codes that have many medical provides. We reject the null hypothesis with 95% confidence. The linear regression line has a slight positive slope, and thus there is a weak positive correlation between life expectancy and number of medical providers per zip code.
